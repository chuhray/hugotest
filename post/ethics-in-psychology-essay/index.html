According to the American Psychological Association, (APA) there are five general principles in which help psychologist maintains professionalism while performing scientific duties. The five general principles are beneficence, fidelity, integrity, justice, and respect for people’s rights. These core principle help guide 12 subsections of proper ethics in research involving human participants. These subsections are in place to protect the participants because they are placing a great amount of trust in the experimenter. They are designed to eliminate discrimination, harassment, and exploitation of subjects. They also ensure that the experimenter considers the various roles and external factors such as third parties.
